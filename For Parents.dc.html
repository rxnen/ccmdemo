For parents

Here's what Wednesday is really doing for your teen

Midrasha is the rare weekly commitment teens defend on their own. One graduate called it his "safe haven" through the hardest stretches of high school; another said it kept her "grounded and supported" through senior-year uncertainty. That's by design — and here's exactly how.

Five things Midrasha gives your teen

🌙

A weekly unplugged reset

Face-to-face friendship with Jewish peers from across the county — a genuine break from screens, school stress, and social pressure, in a warm and safe environment.

📚

A curriculum that meets them where they are

Juniors get a therapist-built stress-management course; seniors prepare for real life after high school. (Grade by grade below.)

🤝

Service that means something

Through Be The Change, teens build yearlong friendships with seniors and lead hands-on projects — earning service hours while living Jewish values.

✡️

Jewish identity that sticks

Not memorization — belonging. Teens leave proud of who they are, with mentors they stay in touch with for years.

🍍

Every Jewish teen belongs

Interfaith families, mixed-heritage kids, no bar/bat mitzvah, no synagogue, no Hebrew — truly all are welcome, and our teens feel it from day one.

Grade by grade

A program that grows with your teen

Every year has its own focus — meeting teens exactly where they are.

8–9

Find your people

A core group of peers from across the county, the foundations of Jewish identity, and the first real Midrasha friendships.

10

Go deeper

Electives, bigger conversations, and the chance to step into leadership — including Be The Change service programs.

11

Rodef Shalom

A stress-management course built by a therapist for the hardest academic year — real tools for a high-pressure junior fall.

12

Ready for what's next

A year of preparing for life after high school: building community, handling money, taking care of themselves, and staying Jewish in college.

The logistics

Where

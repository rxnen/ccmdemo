What a Wednesday night actually feels like

You walk in, grab a donut, and find your friends — kids from a dozen different schools across Contra Costa who, somehow, feel more like your people than anyone you sit with at lunch.

First hour, you're with your grade: real conversations about identity, Israel, stress, life after high school — the stuff that actually matters, with people who get it. Second hour, you pick: Israeli cooking, art, improv, movies, music. Then more hanging out, because nobody's ever in a hurry to leave.

No tests. No grades. No Hebrew required. Just a weekly reset in the middle of the week — one graduate called it "a mid-week Shabbat."

For parents

Here's what Wednesday is really doing for your teen

Midrasha is the rare weekly commitment teens defend on their own. One graduate called it his "safe haven" through the hardest stretches of high school; another said it kept her "grounded and supported" through senior-year uncertainty. That's by design:

🌙

A weekly unplugged reset. Face-to-face friendship with Jewish peers from across the county — a genuine break from screens, school stress, and social pressure.

📚

A curriculum that meets teens where they are. Juniors get "Rodef Shalom," a stress-management course built by a therapist for the hardest academic year. Seniors prepare for real life after high school — community, money, self-care, staying Jewish in college.

🤝

Service that means something. Through Be The Change, teens build yearlong friendships with seniors and lead hands-on projects — earning service hours while living Jewish values.

✡️

Jewish identity that sticks. Not memorization — belonging. Teens leave proud of who they are, with mentors they stay in touch with for years.

🍍

Every Jewish teen belongs. Interfaith families, mixed-heritage kids, no bar/bat mitzvah, no synagogue, no Hebrew — truly all are welcome, and our teens feel it from day one.

Join any year. Any week.

Most teens start in 8th or 9th grade — but plenty join as sophomores, juniors, even seniors. You are not too late, and you won't be the outsider. Ask Lindsay, who joined sophomore year terrified everyone already knew each other: "My expectations couldn't have been more wrong." Or Hannah, who joined as a senior and called it one of the most meaningful years of her life.

You can register any time during the year — or just show up one Wednesday and see for yourself.

Why is our logo a pineapple? 🍍

Pineapples are an old symbol of hospitality — and at Midrasha, every new teen gets a real one. Here's how one graduate remembered his first night:

"I still remember to this day, walking up and grabbing my pineapple while everyone clapped and cheered just for being there."

That's the whole idea. You show up; we celebrate you.
